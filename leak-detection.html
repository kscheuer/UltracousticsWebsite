Application

Ultrasonic Gas Leak Detection

Omnidirectional, gas-agnostic detection of micro-scale leaks through broadband ultrasonic sensing, even in high-noise industrial environments.

Request a Consultation View BROADSONIC Specs

Why Ultrasonic Leak Detection?

Pressurized gas leaks produce broadband ultrasonic emissions as turbulent flow exits through small orifices. These acoustic signatures extend well into the MHz range, far above ambient industrial noise. BROADSONIC captures this full spectrum with 5 MHz bandwidth, enabling detection of leaks that conventional acoustic sensors miss entirely.

Gas-Agnostic by Design

Unlike infrared or optical gas imaging (OGI) methods that rely on molecular absorption of specific gases, ultrasonic detection is fundamentally acoustic. It responds to the turbulent flow itself, not the gas composition. This means BROADSONIC detects leaks regardless of the gas involved: natural gas, compressed air, nitrogen, refrigerants, hydrogen, or any other pressurized medium.

Omnidirectional Sensitivity

With greater than ±60 degrees of omnidirectionality, BROADSONIC does not need to be aimed directly at a leak source. The sensor responds to acoustic energy arriving from a wide range of angles, simplifying deployment and enabling area monitoring without precise positioning.

Performance in High-Noise Environments

Industrial facilities are acoustically noisy, but most of that noise concentrates below 100 kHz. Leak signatures extend to MHz frequencies where the background is quiet. BROADSONIC's 5 MHz bandwidth allows detection algorithms to operate in frequency bands with minimal interference, achieving reliable detection even near compressors, pumps, and other loud machinery.

Non-Contact, Standoff Detection

BROADSONIC operates entirely in air with no physical contact required. The sensor head contains no electrical elements, providing inherent immunity to electromagnetic interference. The fiber-coupled design allows the lightweight 3 g sensor head to be positioned flexibly while the control unit remains in a convenient location up to 3 meters away.
